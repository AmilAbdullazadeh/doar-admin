## How it Works

Bootstrap’s grid system uses a series of containers, rows, and columns to layout and align content. It’s built with flexbox and is fully responsive. Below is an example and an in-depth look at how the grid comes together.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="How it Works">
        <Container>
            <Row>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">One of three columns</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">One of three columns</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">One of three columns</div>
                </Col>
            </Row>
        </Container>
    </Story>
</Preview>

## Setting One Column Width

Auto-layout for flexbox grid columns also means you can set the width of one column and have the sibling columns automatically resize around it.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="Setting One Column Width">
        <Container>
            <Row>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">1 of 3</div>
                </Col>
                <Col sm={5}>
                    <div className="st-bg">2 of 3 (wider)</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">1 of 3</div>
                </Col>
            </Row>
        </Container>
    </Story>
</Preview>

## Gutters

Gutters can be responsively adjusted by breakpoint-specific padding and negative margin utility classes.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="Gutters">

            </Row>
            <Row mb="30px" gutters={20}>

        </Container>

</Preview>

## No Gutters

The gutters between columns in our predefined grid classes can be removed with `noGutter`. This removes the negative margins from `Row` and the horizontal padding from all immediate children columns.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="No Gutters">
        <Container>
            <Row noGutter>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">1 of 3</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">1 of 3</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col>
                    <div className="st-bg">1 of 3</div>
                </Col>
            </Row>
        </Container>
    </Story>
</Preview>

## Reordering

Use `order` prop for controlling the visual order of your content.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="Reordering">
        <Container>
            <Row>
                <Col col order={2}>
                    <div className="st-bg">First, but second</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col order={3}>
                    <div className="st-bg">Second, but last</div>
                </Col>
                <Col col order={1}>
                    <div className="st-bg">Third, but first</div>
                </Col>
            </Row>
        </Container>
    </Story>
</Preview>

## Offsetting Columns

You can offset grid columns in two ways: our responsive `offset` grid classes and our margin utilities.

<Preview withToolbar>
    <Story name="Offsetting Columns">
        <Container>
            <Row>
                <Col sm="4">
                    <div className="st-bg">Col 4</div>
                </Col>
                <Col sm="4" smOffset={4}>
                    <div className="st-bg">Col 4, offset 4</div>
                </Col>
            </Row>
        </Container>
    </Story>
</Preview>
